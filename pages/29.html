It had always been my habit to rise with the
lark, if there was one going up at about nine.
A confirmed botulist, I first arranged with
Flora that there should be seven of the long
stout shapes rosily bursting from the exquisite,
taut but not too elastic brown at breakfast. I
trusted they would not taste of Flora and the
country-green. Then with whetted appetite,
and after an unsatisfactory visit to the spare-
room, I went for a quick stroll among my
flowers. If the West African ordeal beans had
proved a disappointment, at least the broad
ones were giving satisfaction. On that
day—and indeed I was well inspired—I dis-
carded my useless physostigma. I led the
old mineralogist up the garden, if I may
be permitted the expression, and introduced
him to my lobelia and to my pretty lords
and ladies. I wanted to see how the combi-
nation would suit him. I felt I ought to be draw-
ing towards a close ; but one never knew.
