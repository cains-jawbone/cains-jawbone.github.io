I rubbed my eyes and massaged my temples
with pronated finger-tips. Then I fumbled
two aspirin tablets into my mouth : Noel
Coward’s King Charles’s Head. I had a very
bad head. My vis-à-vis hadn’t a bad head,
now I came to consider it, bowed over the
documents. It is a very ungentlemanly narrator is a man? thing
to read a private cigarette case. I became a
trifle abstracted. What, I wondered, would he
have said about an abstracted will? He might
answer to the same name as the man who
sang : “Ah, are you digging on my grave?” Ah, Are You Digging On My Grave? by Thomas Hardy
But a softer fellow I had rarely seen. On
velvet, yes, on velvet I would have trusted
him ; but not on cinders, by no means on
cinders. Yet the keen eyes bent like small
topaz searchlights over the writing. I would get,
I felt, what I wanted from this man. But then I
suddenly remembered the words of the poet :

The golden one is gone from the banquets,
She, beloved of Atimetus, Epigram, by Harriet Monroe
