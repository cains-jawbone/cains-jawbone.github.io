I started to read Hardy’s exquisite production,
and every muscle of my brain was enthralled
until I came to the end. Just such another must
have been the Monk Arnulphus when he
uncorked his ink. His palette gleamed with a
burnished green as bright as a dragon-fly’s
skin : his gold-leaf shone like the robe of a
queen. There could be no slightest doubt.
I would now be able to reap the harvest. And
Ruth would have little gleaning. I thought of
her mother and laughed aloud. All women
become like their mothers. That is their
tragedy. No man does. That’s his. I could not
help echoing Jack’s question : is that clever? The Monk Arnulphus, with a dash of Jim the
Penman. How, I wondered, did I strike him? I knew I should like to. His was obviously a
slow methodical brain, used to pigeon-holing by
type. In that case, I thought I knew the type :
learned in a macabre way, even distinguished.
