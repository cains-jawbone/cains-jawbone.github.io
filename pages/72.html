Of course I was sorry to say good-bye to old
Medehamstede ; but it was pleasant to sit down
and to really find myself alone at last. Those
emotional times were trying to us all. I felt that
my lips were paler than I liked ; but a touch
of Pasquier’s claret soon put me right. Dear old
Pasquier, I had come across him in Paris, at
that little place in the Rue de la Harpe, a street
in which, I have been told, there was a touch of
orderly room even in the disorderly houses.
I opened a magazine and looked hastily
through the last paragraphs of the short
stories. I was all for love ; but fading out on an
embrace never appealed to me. The embrace
in my short stories—and my life was all short
stories, I had come to think—occurred in the
first few words. And afterwards the plot. The
complete novel length looked better. It was
called Savage Conqueror, and I liked that.
